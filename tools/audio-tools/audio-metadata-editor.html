A browser-based MP3 tag editor — no upload, no re-encode

Most online MP3 tag editors accept your file, upload it to a server, run a backend library such as mutagen or id3lib to rewrite the tags, then send the file back. The browser-side approach used here is different — the MP3 file is opened locally, its existing ID3 frames are read by jsmediatags running in JavaScript, you edit the fields in place, then browser-id3-writer constructs a fresh ID3v2.3 tag block and prepends it to your MP3 bytes entirely inside your tab. The audio payload itself is never re-encoded, so the process is light, instant, and lossless.

That also means this is the safer choice for sensitive audio: voice memos, recordings of meetings, unreleased music, podcast drafts. Nothing leaves your device. If the page refreshes, your files are already gone — there's no server-side copy to worry about.

Why edit MP3 tags locally instead of uploading your MP3s to a website?

Upload-based MP3 tag editors send your file to a server you can't audit. For personal recordings or unreleased music, that's a real concern — once a copy of your MP3 lands somewhere else, you no longer fully control it. This browser-side tag editor keeps every byte on your own device. The ID3 read and write happen inside your tab, in JavaScript, and the only thing the server ever sent you was the page itself.
