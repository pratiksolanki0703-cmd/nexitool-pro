How the page removal actually works

This tool parses your page spec — single numbers, ranges like 7-9, or a mix separated by commas — into a list of page indexes, removes them directly from the PDF's internal page tree, and rebuilds the file. The remaining pages and their content are untouched, so there's no quality loss.

A safety check blocks any spec that would remove every page in a file, since a valid PDF always needs at least one page.
