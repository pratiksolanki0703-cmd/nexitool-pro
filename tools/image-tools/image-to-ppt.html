Convert photos to PowerPoint slides without OCR

Some "image to PPT" tools try to extract text from your photos using OCR — useful for scanned documents, but overkill (and slower, and error-prone) when you just want each photo placed cleanly on its own slide. This tool skips OCR entirely: every image you add becomes one picture slide, exactly as it looks.

Reorder slides by dragging the thumbnails, choose a 16:9 or 4:3 layout, and pick how each image should fit its slide — then download a standard .pptx file that opens in PowerPoint, Google Slides, or Keynote.

How it works

  • Upload multiple photos
  • Drag thumbnails to reorder them
  • Choose 16:9 or 4:3 and an image fit mode
  • Click Generate PowerPoint
  • Download your .pptx file

Good for

Photo recaps: turn an event's photos into a shareable slideshow.
Visual reports: drop screenshots or charts straight into slides.
Portfolios: one image per slide, in the order you choose.

Image fit modes

Contain: whole image visible, may leave empty space.
Cover: fills the slide, crops the edges slightly.
Stretch: fills exactly, may distort the aspect ratio.
