Why use a batch compressor instead of an editor?

Opening each photo in an editor to shrink it one at a time doesn't scale past a handful of files. This tool lets you drop in an entire folder, set one quality level, and let the browser process the whole batch — useful for trimming a camera roll before an upload, or getting a set of product photos under a platform's size limit.

Because the compression runs on canvas inside your browser, there's no queue, no waiting on a server, and no copy of your photos sitting on someone else's disk afterward.

How it works

  • Drop in one photo or a whole batch
  • Drag the quality slider to trade off size vs. sharpness
  • Optionally switch the output format
  • Watch each card update with its new size instantly
  • Download one at a time, or grab everything as a ZIP

Why bother shrinking photos?

Pages load faster when the images on them are lighter.
Storage stretches further once duplicates and originals aren't both full-size.
Uploads stop bouncing off size limits on forms and platforms.
Sharing gets quicker over slow or metered connections.
