How the Scam Mail Checker actually works

Paste the raw content of a suspicious email — the header lines, the body, or both. The AI reads it for classic phishing and fraud signals: urgency language, mismatched sender domains, requests for money or credentials, suspicious links, and poor grammar patterns typical of scam campaigns.

It returns a 0-100 risk score, a plain verdict (Safe, Suspicious, Likely Scam, or Confirmed Scam), and a bulleted list of the specific reasons behind the score — explained in whichever language you choose from the slider or your own custom entry.

When this is worth doing

Unexpected "urgent" emails: anything demanding immediate action.
Requests for money or credentials: always worth a second check.
Unfamiliar senders: especially ones claiming to be a known company.
Suspicious links or attachments: before you click anything.
